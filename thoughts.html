Thoughts

A few poems — written whenever the words came.

Tulips

They are bright and red now,
But with time they will wither,
The petals will drop one by one,
And everything which makes them pretty,
Will disappear,
The only thing that will remain,
Is the memory of the fragrance they leave behind,
It is not even the fragrance,
Just a vague memory of the fragrance,
Which filled this room once.

Beginnings

I woke up around 7 am,
Was feeling dizzy,
Because of insomnia hitting again,
I looked out the window,
An unusual winter morning,
Was waiting for me outside.
I got up,
Opened the door,
And entered into the balcony,
The sun was shining bright,
No mist,
No haziness,
And the dark clouds,
Which were hovering over my head yesterday,
Appeared to be raining somewhere else,
The air was surprisingly fresh,
And warm,
The season was changing.
Days full of uncertainties,
And doubts,
Were all set to leave,
The light was trying hard,
To find its way to my room,
I went back inside,
Leaving the doors wide open.

Sea

There is a place beyond this sea,
Where the horizon ends,
Where this sea,
Which looks endless from here,
Ends,
There exist a shore that,
Even the sea can't breach,
I must go to that place,
Where the sea meets the land,
I must go to that place,
Where I can find my feet,
And walk,
And run,
And thrive,
I must go to that place,
Where it doesn't feel so cold,
Where the winds are not so harsh,
Where the sun feels warm,
Where the tides cannot crush me,
I must keep going,
Because,
There is such a place,
Beyond this endless sea.

Flow

Let life flow like a river,
Do what needs to be done,
If it goes wrong,
Think about it,
Regret it,
Keep it in your heart for a long long time,
Visit those memories every now and then,
And cry maybe if it hurts too much,
You can't undo what has already been done,
Because the river doesn't flow in reverse,
The only thing you can do is,
Move on,
And flow again.

Goodbye Edinburgh

You visit any city,
You are not the same person as before,
You leave a little part of you there,
And at the same time,
Take a little part of the city with you.
I am happy to be heading closer to home,
But also sad,
That I may never get to meet the little part of me,
That I am leaving here,
All I can do is to cherish that tiny-tiny part of the city,
That I am taking with me,
And I will indeed cherish it for a very long time.

Gone

You wake up one day,
And realize that a part of your life is gone,
A person who was with you till yesterday, is gone,
You suddenly have an extra hour in your life,
Which didn't exist till yesterday,
The person who consumed that extra hour, is gone,
You don't know what to do with the time they left behind,
You don't know what to do with the void they left behind,
You can only hope that someone else will fill that void some day,
Maybe someone else will fill that void one day,
But that person who was there till yesterday, is now gone,
Forever.

I saw you there today

I saw you lying there today,
That's the palest I've seen you,
That's the thinnest, I have ever seen you Grandma,
There you were,
Lying on the big white bed,
Covered in white linen sheets,
With your knees dipped into your stomach,
You look so small,
How did you become so thin, Grandma?
You were lying there silently,
So silent that I couldn't hear your breathing,
I miss your rampant breathing now,
It gives me the assurance that you are here.
It makes me feel your existence,
I am standing at the door,
But I do not have the courage,
To go inside,
And see you in this state, Grandma,
But they pushed me inside,
Saying this might be the last opportunity,
How can this be the last opportunity, Grandma?
